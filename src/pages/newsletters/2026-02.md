<h3>Profit Sharing Report from Reggie</h3>
<p>With bonuses and raises hitting (or NOT hitting) our checking accounts this time of year, it is worth remembering how much we have all made as a company.</p><p><a href="https://www.reuters.com/business/finance/bofa-award-stock-worth-1-billion-non-executive-employees-2026-01-20/">Bank of America recently awarded $1 billion worth of stock</a> to their employees as a reward for high profits. They are only the second largest US bank; we are the first.</p><p>Therefore, I propose a 25% profit share to be split equally among all 300,000 employees. As far as I know, no part of JPMC currently does anything like this.</p><p>According to the quarterly earnings reports, here's how that would've looked in 2025. The "Profit Share" column is what you personally would have received before taxes, either by the quarter or by the whole year.</p><table><tbody><tr><td></td><td><strong>Net Income</strong></td><td><strong>25% of Net</strong></td><td><strong>Profit Share</strong></td></tr><tr><td><strong>Q1</strong></td><td>$14.6 billion</td><td>$3.65 billion</td><td>$12,000</td></tr><tr><td><strong>Q2</strong></td><td>$15 billion</td><td>$3.75 billion</td><td>$12,500</td></tr><tr><td><strong>Q3</strong></td><td>$14.4 billion</td><td>$3.6 billion</td><td>$12,000</td></tr><tr><td><strong>Q4</strong></td><td>$13 billion</td><td>$3.25 billion</td><td>$11,000</td></tr><tr><td><strong>2025 Total</strong></td><td><strong>$57 billion</strong></td><td><strong>$14.25 billion</strong></td><td><strong><em>$47,500</em></strong></td></tr></tbody></table><p>Compare that $47,500 with three other numbers: first, the amount of money you were paid over the course of 2025; second, the amount of bonus you received (if any); third, the amount of raise you were given (if any).</p><p>Remember $47,500 every time someone tells you the bank doesn't have money for raises or bonuses.</p><p>Think about the difference an extra $47,500 would make in your life. Even after taxes, that's at least $28,500 hitting your bank account. Imagine what you could do for yourself, your family, your friends, or your favorite charities if you had that lump sum.</p><p>Then remember that instead of giving you the money that you are rightfully owed, the firm gives the CEO and other high level executives (who are already set for life!) multi-million dollar bonuses and claims to have too little money for raises or bonuses for the rest of us.</p><p>It's time to get angry. You are victim to a grave injustice.</p><h3><strong><em>YOU DESERVE BETTER!</em></strong></h3>
<p>The JWA wants to get you what you are owed. Simply <a href="https://discord.gg/BZA3pxppq9">join our Discord</a> to be connected with your likeminded coworkers and learn how to right this wrong.</p><p><br></p>
<h3>Winter Themed Poetry from BK and Beth
</h3>
<p>Each night upon our trek through rain, or snow, or just plain cold;<br>with street lamps glowing overhead and mem’ries growing old,<br>of work/life balance being fair, of safety drawn from steady work,<br>of health care when we needed, and of raises big and bold;<br>nor did dreadful “right-sizings” ’round every seventh payday lurk;
</p>
<p>The journey home allows us time to ponder rights and freedoms lost<br>amid this dark and chilly workers’ winter to the dragon’s ever-growing hoard<br>maximizing owners’ short-term gain regardless of the cost<br>to families and citizens and ordinary folks whose time and lives are poured<br>upon this tow’ring enterprise that is, with cursive letters so embossed,<br>as <em>J.P. Morgan Chase</em>, a member of the banking board.
</p>
<p>As Earth upon her course must soon leave winter’s cold embrace,<br>let workers near and far join hands as spring’s fresh golden bloom!<br>Standing side by side as one with courage and with grace,<br>we’ll change the course of history: capricious greed sent to its doom.
</p>
<p>This year we claim back what has always been ours:
</p>
<ul>
    <li>Time for those we love.
    </li>
    <li>Fair wages that rise with profits rather than dwindling through inflation.
    </li>
    <li>The right to be heard clearly, not pushed off to the side.
    </li>
    <li>A proper training pipeline, both for ourselves and our direct managers.
    </li>
    <li>Freedom from pressure to sacrifice what is right for what is right-now.
    </li>
    <li>Freedom from arbitrary treatment and weaponized “managerial discretion”.
    </li>
    <li>Just-cause requirement for adverse HR actions consistent with Union custom.
    </li>
</ul>
<p>As we gather close this winter, let us lift the lights of our hearts as one, wrap ourselves in the strength of unity, and give ourselves the gift of us. The ornaments of our hopes shine brighter when we choose togetherness.
</p>
